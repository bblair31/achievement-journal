### 2. Many users with identical match scores, means pagination results are non-deterministic
This is also a factor in "duplicate" candidates showing up. And why the example duplicate user in that thread randomly appears and disappears.

With the previous sort, when users have identical scores, it causes issues with pagination. **Across different page offsets (i.e. 10 per page) BigQuery will return an arbitrary order if you don't give it any unique sort keys**. So it could return the same user(s) across two different pages.

In the [example from the slack thread](https://admin.workup.health/ny-cancer/rediscovery/rec42zKehTYHaAFAx) more than 10 users have the exact same score of 9.2. Followed by more than 10 users that have the exact same score of 8.5. So basically BigQuery was randomly selecting 10 of those users for each page/query they _**could**_ belong on.

What I'm adding here is tertiary sorts by `last_activity_date` and `user_id` to guarantee uniqueness and predictable pagination
